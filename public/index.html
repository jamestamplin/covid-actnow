Options

As you can see, your state hospital system is about to be highly over capacity. There are roughly three options:

OPTION 1: LOCKDOWN & OPTIMIZE FOR SURVIVAL RATES

60,000 Dead in the US, 24 Months to TRUE Normalcy, 15% Hit to Economy, 1 Year to Recovery

We move to contain the disease by locking down, similar to South Korea, within the next 48 hours. This likely means getting citizens to remain in their homes for about a month, while scaling up healthcare infrastructure. In conjunction, we an aid bill that couples tax breaks for businesses, financial support for individuals, and free widespread testing and health care is needed.

Results: By the end of April, the disease is contained, and has killed less people than the flu does annually (~60,000 in the US). The US economy contracts by about 15%, but recovers within a year. Aggressive testing must continue at the US border for several years, until a vaccine has been developed, dragging out a true return to true normalcy. We risk facing periodic virus resurgence, as we have not built “herd immunity”. Pundits speculate about what they see as “an overreaction.”

OPTION 2: LET THE DISEASE SPREAD & OPTIMIZE FOR “HERD IMMUNITY”

10,000,000+ Dead in the US, 3-4 Months to Normalcy, 15-30% Hit to Economy, 1-1.5 Years to Recovery

We do nothing (or effectively nothing). The disease rockets through the population, infecting 70-80% of us.

Results: Due to the uncontrolled nature of the disease, and the overwhelming of the hospital system, 3-5% of those infected die (the current numbers in Italy are 6.6%), resulting in >10 million deaths in less than 6 months. The public achieves herd immunity by June/July, and business returns to normal. The US economy contracts by about 15-30%, but recovers within 12-18 months.

OPTION 3: FIND A “HAPPY MEDIUM”, AVOID THE HEALTHCARE SYSTEM BEING OVERRUN

3,000,000+ Dead in the US, 6-12 Months to Normalcy, 30-50% Hit to Economy, 1.5-2 Years to Recovery

This is not a single option, but really a range of options. Both government and corporations gradually implement strong but voluntary measures to increase “social distancing,” over the next 2-4 weeks, and reduce the rate at which people are infected such that the healthcare system is not overrun (ie #flattenthecurve).

Results: While 70% of the population is infected in 2020, we avoid a worst case scenario, and achieve a death rate of about 1-1.5%. >3 million people die in the US (50-100x the flu). However, the US economy is slowed to a crawl for 6-12 months, contracting 30-50%, and taking 18-24 months to fully recover. We may implement an aid bill to help offset the cost to companies and the public, as outlined above. We now enjoy “herd immunity,” and can fully reopen borders at the end of the year.
